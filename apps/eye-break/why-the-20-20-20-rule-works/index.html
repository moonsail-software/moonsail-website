20-20-20 Rule for Computer Eye Strain: Why It Works

If you work at a computer all day, your eyes spend hours locked into close focus. The 20-20-20 rule is one of the simplest habits for reducing computer eye strain and digital eye strain: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds. It is easy, free, and much more realistic when you use an eye break app for Android to keep you on schedule.

Why screens make your eyes feel tired

Computer work keeps your eyes doing the same close-up job for long stretches. That constant near-focus can leave your eyes feeling dry, tired, or slow to refocus when you look up from the screen. Even when the symptoms are mild, computer eye strain and digital eye strain build up over a long workday.

What the 20-20-20 rule does

The rule interrupts uninterrupted near work. When you look into the distance, the focusing muscles in your eyes get a brief reset instead of staying locked on text, code, spreadsheets, or design tools for hours at a time.

That is why the 20-20-20 rule is so commonly recommended for people who spend most of the day on a laptop, desktop monitor, or tablet.

Why 20 seconds matters

A quick glance across the room is better than nothing, but it usually is not enough to break your working rhythm or your visual focus. Holding the distant gaze for 20 seconds gives the pause enough structure to become a real eye break instead of an accidental distraction.

Why consistency is the hard part

Most people do not ignore eye breaks because they disagree with the habit. They ignore them because focused work makes time disappear. Meetings run long, coding sessions stretch out, and one more email becomes 45 more minutes at the screen.

That is where a dedicated 20-20-20 app helps. Instead of relying on memory, you offload the schedule to a background reminder that tells you exactly when it is time to pause.
